Nruthyopasana-2015

[Organised under Grant-in-aid by Ministry of Culture, Govt. of India]

Conducted on February 21st, 22nd and 23rd of 2015, organized at Jagan Mohan Palace, Mysore.

Day 1, 21st Feb 2015:

The festival was formally inaugurated Dr. Vasundhara Doraswamy, Millennium Artist awarded by UNO, accompanied by Dr. R.S. Nandha Kumar, Vocalist, Music Composer, Mysore whose address to the students was a great inspiration for all of us.The formal function also witnessed the felicitation program of our Dance Guru Vid. Sri R. Kannan, Senior Dance Teacher, Mysore, who has contributed enough to the Dance field by teaching Dance in a rural area like T. Narasipura for more than Two decades. Talented Artists of our Trust were awarded a Certificate Excellence in honour of their high academic performance, in order to support upcoming artists and were strongly encouraged to contribute to the growth of the Trust.

The commencement of this festival will be accomplished by the performance of the Vid. Sri K Sandesh Bhargav, senior disciple of Karnataka Kala Tilaka Dr. Vasundhara Doraswamy. The long recital holds the traditional base of Marga.The highlight of this portrayal clings to the character ‘Karna’ from the very famous Pampa Bharatha, beautifully designed into a Padavarna. ‘Bravery or the sacrifice should we salute, as Karna makes his alms to Indra’ are among the first few lines. Karna choosing the point of no return to fight Pandavas, the strategies adopted by Krishna to witness the victory of the Pandavas during the great Kurukshetra war and the discrepancy hat occurred when the mark of truth, sacrifice and bravery, breathed his last are fragments of this unique interpretation by Pampa, Kannada poet of 10th Century.

Sandesh has wonderfully presented the Bharathanatyam Margam performance. His soothing performance with extraordinary grace on face, energetic Nrutta and charming expressions stolen the heart of audience. This was the highlight of the performance by Sandesh Bhargav.

Day 2, 22nd Feb 2015:

It’s a group production of nearly Twenty Artists, presented a Dance Drama: “Vishnu Praseeda-Mahishapuravasi” narrating the mythology of the holy Vishnu Temples in and around Mysore. Stories behind the 10 various Vishnu temples of Mysore has been studied and beautifully scripted by Dr. Jyothishanker and wonderfully choreographed by Guru Sandesh Bhargav. This new and unique performance was appreciated by scholars and audience.

Day 3, 23rd Feb 2015: Last Day is the Platform for Budding Artists (below 14 years), who have stepped into the art field with a dream of a magnificent future. They have showcased their talents through a Bharatanatyam recital. Nearly Seventy Child artists mainly from rural areas around Mysore stepped their small feet on the stage. This is mainly to create an esteemed platform to these budding little dancers and motivate them.

Art Critic Mrs. Devanaampriya has mentioned below points in her review.

  • "Dr. Jyothi Shankar, who composed the padavarnam from the extract of Pampa Bharata, was impeccable and blended with Dr. Vasundhara’s spotless choreography executed flawlessly by Sandesh"
  • "All the stories were well presented with appropriate bhava and rasa by the students. Good effort was made in portraying the characters which captivated the audience"
  • "The children on the stage had a good energy level, were lively, synchronized well and moreover had beautiful smiles which captured the hearts of the audience"
