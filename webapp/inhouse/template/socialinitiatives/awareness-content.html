Kala Sandesha Prathishtana has been conducting workshops and summercamps for children since to bring the awareness about the Indian classical art forms.

A subject, various perspectives involving discussions and debating over the methods of learning and its pros and cons, which also encircles the tools that aid in better understanding and interpretation of that subject is all that comes under the term, Workshop. It remains effective in improving one’s skills by providing practical exposure and also serves as a center for exchange of thoughts and opinions. Tala and Laya are the two vital components of Bharathanatyam. To build a strong platform of these two upon which can dance flaunt, Kala Sandesha Prathishtana® organized several workshops.

The very thought of summer for kids, is Fun. During those days off from school, they mostly involve themselves in the activities that please their passion. In present situation, children are well in competition with the adults in trying out new things. Only after they’re grown will they be able to justify their efforts. Thus, to trigger those creative corners of the young minds, Kala Sandesha Prathishtana® came up with Summer Camps, from 10 years now. This was ever since a righteous attempt to provide the students of the institution with knowledge, by allowing the faculty members and at times other scholars to teach them.

Workshop - 2016

Laya and Jathi Workshop (14th,15th of February 2016 and 11th,12th of March 2016)

In the year 2016, two of such workshops named ‘Laya and Jathis’ were undertaken. One of which was scheduled on 14th and 15th of February 2016 by Vid. Sri K Sandesh Bhargav, Founder of KSP, in J.P.Nagar, Mysuru. About 30 students participated in this workshop. Most of them were of very young age with high grasping ability. The second workshop of this kind was driven by senior faculty members of the institution in 11th and 12th of March 2016 involving 200 students.

The workshops began with a lecture on ‘How tala and laya hold importance in dance’. Soon after, the basic techniques that help in better practice of Tala and Laya were taught. The session was further set into motion by adopting different Jathis like Trishra, Chathurashra, Khanda, Mishra and Sankeerna and, inserting the former learnt techniques in them in different speeds, was for the first day. The second day started by practicing the Day 1’s learning and dealt more with the usage of same fashion/process in the Alankaras of Karnataka Classical Music. The young artists enthusiastically took part in both the workshop marking its success.

Summer camps - 2014

Three Summer Camps happened to be a part of 2014 which were conducted in Mysuru, T. Narasipura and Talakadu respectively on the same time. The faculty of KSP took charge of tutoring the participants.

Alanahalli Camp (15th of April to 15th of May, 2014)

Mysuru Camp was scheduled from 15th of April to 15th of May, 2014 wherein 30-40 candidates actively participated under the guidance of Vidushi Kum. Anusha G.M., a senior faculty member. About 20 students turned up from various neighboring cities, resided for a month to engage themselves in the Camp. Dr. Savitha, a popular physician inaugurated the Summer Camp which successfully made its progression for a month. Dr. Subbe Gowda, a famous Psychiatrist in the city motivated the students on, ‘how health and knowledge play their duo in the all-round development of an individual’ on the valedictory day. The camp aided in elevating the knowledge successfully till the final day.

T.Narasipura Camp (started 15th of April, 2014)

Summer camp in T Narasipura, a rural area neighboring Mysuru also started from the 15th of April, 2014 and went on for a month’s time. Another senior faculty Vidushi Smt Sindhu Niranjan drove this camp of nearly 50 students, on a whole. Dr. Poornima, a renowned physician inaugurated this camp on the first day. Students were provided with the basic training on the initial days. Moving forward, they were equipped with a bunch of short duration dance bits which were performed by them as a part of the finale. ‘Rural talents are no less in competition with the contemporary world’ was the outcome drawn from this Summer Camp. Kala Sandesha Prathishtana® hence looks forward to creating more of such opportunities to nurture rural young talents.

Talakadu Camp (15th April to 15th May)

The third and the last camp of 2014 was held in Talakadu for one month from 15th April to 15th May. Sri T Purushotham, a retired Syndicate Bank Manager inaugurated and shared his optimistic thoughts and opinions about the Summer Camp. The students enthusiastically engaged in learning, practicing and performing, marking the successful end to it.
